# Hypertrophy

## Definition
The hypertrophy superclass (HYP) covers ECG evidence of increased chamber mass,
including left and right ventricular hypertrophy and atrial enlargement.

## Left ventricular hypertrophy
Increased left ventricular mass produces tall left-sided and deep right-sided
QRS voltages. Common voltage criteria include:
- Sokolow-Lyon: the S wave in V1 plus the R wave in V5 or V6 exceeding 35 mm.
- Cornell: the R wave in aVL plus the S wave in V3 exceeding sex-specific
  thresholds.
A strain pattern of ST depression and T-wave inversion in the lateral leads
often accompanies established hypertrophy.

## Right ventricular hypertrophy
Increased right ventricular mass shifts forces rightward and anteriorly:
- A dominant R wave in V1 with R greater than S.
- Right axis deviation.
- A deep S wave persisting into the lateral precordial leads.

## Atrial enlargement
- Left atrial enlargement: a broad, notched P wave in lead II and a deep
  terminal negative component in V1.
- Right atrial enlargement: a tall, peaked P wave in lead II.

## Interpretation notes
Voltage criteria are specific but not sensitive, and voltage alone can mislead
in thin or young patients. The combination of voltage criteria with a strain
pattern and supportive axis findings increases diagnostic confidence.
