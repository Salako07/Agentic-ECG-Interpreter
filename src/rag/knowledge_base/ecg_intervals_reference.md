# ECG Interval Reference

## Purpose
Interval durations are among the most reproducible ECG measurements and anchor
much of diagnostic interpretation. This reference summarizes normal ranges and
the significance of deviation.

## PR interval
Normal 120 to 200 milliseconds, measured from the start of the P wave to the
start of the QRS. Prolongation defines first-degree AV block. Shortening may
occur with pre-excitation.

## QRS duration
Normal 80 to 120 milliseconds. Widening to 120 milliseconds or more suggests a
bundle branch block, a ventricular rhythm, or a paced beat. Fragmentation may
reflect scar.

## QT interval
Measured from the start of the QRS to the end of the T wave and corrected for
heart rate as QTc. Prolongation raises the risk of torsades de pointes and may
be congenital, drug-induced, or metabolic. Shortening is less common.

## Heart rate and RR interval
Rate is derived from the RR interval between successive beats. Below 60 beats
per minute is bradycardia; above 100 is tachycardia. RR variability underlies
heart rate variability measures.

## Measurement notes
At lower sampling rates each sample spans more time, so fine interval
measurement is coarser and benefits from aggregation across multiple beats.
Using a robust central estimate across beats reduces the effect of any single
mis-delineated complex.
