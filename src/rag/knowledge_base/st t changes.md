# ST/T Changes

## Definition
The ST/T change superclass (STTC) covers abnormalities of the ST segment and
T wave that are not specifically diagnostic of acute infarction, including
ischemic changes, strain patterns, and nonspecific repolarization changes.

## Common patterns
- ST-segment depression: horizontal or downsloping depression suggests
  subendocardial ischemia; may also reflect strain or drug effect.
- T-wave inversion: may indicate ischemia, prior infarction, strain from
  ventricular hypertrophy, or a nonspecific process.
- Nonspecific ST-T changes: subtle deviations not meeting criteria for a
  specific diagnosis, common and often of uncertain significance.

## Contributing causes
Repolarization abnormalities have many causes beyond coronary ischemia,
including ventricular hypertrophy with strain, electrolyte disturbance (notably
potassium and calcium), medication effects such as digoxin, and conduction
abnormalities that secondarily alter repolarization.

## Interpretation notes
ST/T changes are sensitive but not specific. Their diagnostic value increases
when they are localized to a coronary territory, dynamic across serial
tracings, or accompanied by symptoms. Isolated nonspecific changes frequently
require clinical correlation.
