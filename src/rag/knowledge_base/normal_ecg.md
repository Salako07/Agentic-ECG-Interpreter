# Normal ECG

## Definition
A normal ECG (superclass NORM) shows normal sinus rhythm with no diagnostic
abnormality of morphology, intervals, axis, or repolarization. It is the
reference against which all pathological patterns are compared.

## Normal sinus rhythm criteria
- Rate between 60 and 100 beats per minute.
- A P wave precedes every QRS complex, and a QRS follows every P wave.
- P waves are upright in leads I and II and inverted in aVR.
- The PR interval is constant from beat to beat.

## Normal interval reference ranges
- PR interval: 120 to 200 milliseconds.
- QRS duration: 80 to 120 milliseconds.
- QT interval: rate-dependent; corrected QT (QTc) below roughly 440 ms in men
  and 460 ms in women.

## Normal axis
The normal frontal-plane QRS axis lies between -30 and +90 degrees. The QRS is
predominantly positive in leads I and II.

## Interpretation notes
Absence of a diagnostic abnormality does not exclude a rhythm abnormality that
is classified separately. A tracing may be morphologically normal yet show a
rhythm finding. When all diagnostic criteria for the abnormal superclasses are
absent, the tracing is reported as normal.
